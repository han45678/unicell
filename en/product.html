What is stem cell?

stem cells are undifferentiated cells that can differentiate into various types of cells and divide indefinitely to produce more of the same stem cell. Stem cells has two main functions:
1. Self-renew: Capable of proliferating, multiplying and regenerating cells to provide more cells to the organs and help modulate immunity.
2. Differentiate: Capable of differentiating into cells required in the organs or tissues to repair or regenerate injured tissues.

成體幹細胞

取決於器官或組織，仍具有優良的分化再生能力來源取得也相對容易為現今臨床研究的主要對象。

胚胎幹細胞

亦稱做全能幹細胞，由受精卵分化而成，具備分化成各種組織器官細胞的能力，該類幹細胞可發展出人體的所有細胞，但具有諸多道德爭議,並未在目前的主研究領域內。

Umbilical cord stem cells

Stem cells can be classified into HSCs and MSCs.

Hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs)

Highly capable of self-regeneration and differentiation to provide blood and immune cells, such as red blood cells, platelets, white blood cells and lymphocytes.

Mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs)

Capable of differentiating into neural cells, skeletal cells, lipid cells, liver cells, etc.

Cell Source (species)

Autologous stem cells

Cells derived from the patient’s own stem cells,
e.g. autologous lipids

Allogeneic stem cells

Donor is of the same species as the patient,
e.g. umbilical cord blood

Xenogeneic stem cells

Donor is from a different species than the patient,
e.g. sheep, shark
